The English word "Christmas" is a shortened form of "Christ's Mass".

The word is recorded as Crīstesmæsse in 1038 and Cristes-messe in 1131.

Crīst (genitive Crīstes) is from Greek Khrīstos (Χριστός), a translation of Hebrew Māšîaḥ "Messiah", meaning "anointed"

and mæsse is from Latin missa, the celebration of the Eucharist.
